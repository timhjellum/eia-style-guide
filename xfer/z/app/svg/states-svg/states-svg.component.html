Notes, Sources, and Related Links

End-use consumption by source, excluding losses

  • These estimates include primary energy and electricity consumption, but exclude electrical system losses associated with the generation, transmission, and distribution of electricity. Other energy transformation losses, other than electrical system losses, are included.
  • End-use consumption of coal, natural gas, petroleum, and renewable energy reflect the heat content of energy consumed directly by end users, not by the electric power sector. See SEDS technical notes for heat content assumptions by source.
  • End-use consumption of electricity is converted from kilowatthours (kWh) to British thermal units (Btu) by applying the heat content of electricity, 3,412 Btu per kWh.
  • End-use consumption of petroleum includes all petroleum products: asphalt and road oil, aviation gasoline, distillate fuel oil, hydrocarbon gas liquids (HGL), jet fuel, kerosene, lubricants, motor gasoline (including fuel ethanol blended into motor gasoline), petroleum coke, residual fuel oil, and other petroleum products consumed by the end-use sectors.
  • End-use consumption of renewable energy includes conventional hydroelectric power, biomass (wood and biomass waste, and losses and co-products from fuel ethanol production), geothermal, solar, and wind energy consumed by the end-use sectors.
  • Total consumption is adjusted to eliminate double-counting of supplemental gaseous fuels, which are included in both natural gas and the other fossil fuels from which they are mostly derived, but should be counted only once in the total.

Electric power sector consumption

  • Electric power sector comprises electricity-only and combined-heat-and-power (CHP) plants within NAICS 22 category whose primary business is to sell electricity, or electricity and useful thermal output, to the public.
  • Electric power sector consumption includes the energy consumed within the state to generate electricity and/or useful thermal output from plants, inclusive of generation losses. See SEDS technical notes for heat content assumptions by source.
  • Net interstate flows of electricity include the electrical system losses associated with the electricity flowing into or out of a state.
  • Net imports/exports of electricity are electricity from/to Canada and Mexico converted from kilowatthours (kWh) to British thermal units (Btu) by applying the heat content of electricity, 3,412 Btu per kWh.

End-use consumption by sector

  • Commercial sector: Includes energy consumed by businesses; federal, state, and local governments; other private and public organizations, such as religious, social, or fraternal groups; institutional living quarters; sewage treatment facilities; and generators that produce electricity and/or useful thermal output primarily to support the activities of the above-mentioned commercial establishments.
  • Industrial sector: Includes energy consumed for manufacturing (NAICS codes 31-33); agriculture, forestry, fishing, and hunting (NAICS code 11); mining, including oil and gas extraction (NAICS code 21); construction (NAICS code 23); and generators that produce electricity and/or useful thermal output primarily to support the above-mentioned industrial activities.
  • Residential sector: Includes energy used for space heating, water heating, air conditioning, lighting, refrigeration, cooking, and running a variety of other appliances in the living quarters of private households.
  • Transportation sector: Includes energy used by automobiles; trucks; buses; motorcycles; trains, subways, and other rail vehicles; aircraft; and ships, barges, and other waterborne vehicles whose primary purpose is transporting people and/or goods from one physical location to another. Vehicles whose primary purpose is not transportation (e.g., construction cranes and bulldozers, farming vehicles, and warehouse forklifts) are classified in the sector of their primary use. Also includes natural gas used in the operation of natural gas pipelines.
Sum of components may not equal total due to independent rounding.
